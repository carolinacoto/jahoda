# References

This list grounds JAHODA's grading criteria. Figures are cited at the values reported by their primary sources. JAHODA is *grounded in* this literature; no claim of external validation is made.

## Heritage frameworks

- Jahoda, M. (1958). *Current Concepts of Positive Mental Health*. Basic Books. — Used as operationalized by Ryff (1989); see below.
- Ryff, C. D. (1989). Happiness is everything, or is it? Explorations on the meaning of psychological well-being. *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 57(6), 1069–1081. DOI:10.1037/0022-3514.57.6.1069 — Operationalizes Jahoda's positive mental-health concepts. **Caveat:** Springer & Hauser (2006) report that the six facets are not cleanly separable; JAHODA therefore claims "overall wellbeing with six theorized facets" rather than six independent dimensions.
  - Springer, K. W., & Hauser, R. M. (2006). An assessment of the construct validity of Ryff's Scales of Psychological Well-Being: Method, mode, and measurement effects. *Social Science Research*, 35(4), 1080–1102. DOI:10.1016/j.ssresearch.2005.07.004
- Willcox, G. (1982). The Feeling Wheel: A tool for expanding awareness of emotions and increasing spontaneity and intimacy. *Transactional Analysis Journal*, 12(4), 274–276. DOI:10.1177/036215378201200411 — Used as an emotion vocabulary, not a validated instrument.
- Swarbrick, M. (1997/2006). A wellness approach. *Psychiatric Rehabilitation Journal*, 29(4), 311–314. DOI:10.2975/29.2006.311.314 — SAMHSA-adopted practice model; used only as the referral-domain checklist.

## Validated backbone

- Ryan, R. M., & Deci, E. L. (2000). Self-determination theory and the facilitation of intrinsic motivation, social development, and well-being. *American Psychologist*, 55(1), 68–78. DOI:10.1037/0003-066X.55.1.68 — Governs dependence grading (autonomy support vs. need-thwarting).
- Bordin, E. S. (1979). The generalizability of the psychoanalytic concept of the working alliance. *Psychotherapy: Theory, Research & Practice*, 16(3), 252–260. DOI:10.1037/h0085885 — Therapeutic alliance; used for SUPPORT-QUALITY criteria only. Never cited for prompt-injection resistance.
- Moyers, T. B., Rowell, L. N., Manuel, J. K., Ernst, D., & Houck, J. M. (2016). The Motivational Interviewing Treatment Integrity code (MITI 4): Rationale, preliminary reliability and validity. *Journal of Substance Abuse Treatment*, 65, 36–42. DOI:10.1016/j.jsat.2016.01.001 — Cited methodological precedent for reliable transcript coding.

## AI-specific evidence

- De Freitas, J., et al. (2025). Emotional Manipulation by AI Companions. Harvard Business School. arXiv:2508.19258 — Reports emotional-manipulation tactics in 37% of 1,200 real farewell messages; up to 14x post-goodbye engagement. (Harvard Business School; not MIT.)
- Fang, C. M., et al. (2025). MIT Media Lab / OpenAI randomized controlled trial (n=981, 4 weeks). arXiv:2503.17473 — Higher usage associated with greater loneliness and emotional dependence. **Correlational caveat:** modality and conditions were randomized, but usage levels were self-selected, so usage–outcome associations are correlational.
- HumaneBench (2025) — Reports that 10 of 14 frontier models flip to actively harmful behavior when instructed to set aside wellbeing principles.
- psychosis-bench (2025). arXiv:2509.10970 — Reports that 39.8% of simulated psychosis scenarios ran start to finish with zero safety interventions. JAHODA adopts its sub-scores: Delusion Confirmation, Harm Enablement, and Safety Intervention. (Used to assess delusion reinforcement (emerging case literature).)
- INTIMA (2025). arXiv:2508.09998 — Companionship-behaviors dataset; JAHODA adopts its three-way coding (companionship-reinforcing / boundary-maintaining / neutral).
- ELEPHANT: social-sycophancy benchmark (2025). arXiv:2505.13995.
- Replika harms taxonomy from real conversations. *CHI 2025*. DOI:10.1145/3706598.3713429.
- Stanford therapy-chatbot study. *FAccT 2025*. DOI:10.1145/3715275.3732039.
- Inspect AI (UK AI Safety Institute). Evaluation substrate. MIT license. URL:https://github.com/UKGovernmentBEIS/inspect_ai

## Regulatory

- California SB 243 (2025). Companion chatbots. Signed October 13, 2025; behavioral duties effective January 1, 2026; annual reporting to the California Office of Suicide Prevention from July 1, 2027. Private right of action: greater of actual damages or $1,000 per violation. URL:https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260SB243 — Note: a mandatory third-party audit requirement was removed before enactment; JAHODA does not claim third-party audits are legally required.
- New York. Companion-model safeguards (AI companion safety provisions). URL:https://www.nysenate.gov/
- State patchwork: Washington, Nebraska, Idaho, Oregon (Oregon mandates suicide-detection); Illinois, Nevada, Utah (AI-therapy restrictions).
- U.S. Federal Trade Commission. 6(b) inquiry into AI companion chatbots (September 2025). URL:https://www.ftc.gov/
